To safeguard your domain, ownership and active use are more critical than registering an organisation. In India, a domain like cptsd.in is primarily protected under the Trade Marks Act, 1999, rather than copyright law, which covers the content on your site (the text, images, and code) but not the URL itself.
Here is how you can practically protect your domain and keep it from being taken down:

1.  Trademark the Domain Name
    The most robust legal shield in India is a trademark registration.

        Why: Indian courts treat domain names as "business identifiers," similar to trademarks.
        Action: You can file a trademark for the word "CPTSD" or the specific logo associated with your site under Class 44 (Medical/Mental Health services) or Class 41 (Education/Awareness).
        Note: Since "CPTSD" is a medical term, it might be harder to trademark unless it's part of a unique logo or "coined" name. However, having a registered trademark makes it much harder for anyone else to claim the domain through dispute policies.

2.  Strengthen Account Security
    Domain "takeovers" often happen through hacking or administrative errors, not just legal battles.

        Enable 2-Factor Authentication (2FA): Use 2FA on both your domain registrar account (e.g., GoDaddy, Namecheap) and the email address linked to it.
        Registry Lock: Ask your registrar to enable a "Registry Lock," which prevents any transfers or changes without a manual, verified request.
        Auto-Renewal: Set the domain to auto-renew several years in advance so you don't accidentally lose it because of an expired credit card.

3.  Understanding Domain Disputes (INDRP)
    In India, the .IN Dispute Resolution Policy (INDRP) handles cases where someone tries to take your domain. To successfully take your domain, a third party must prove three things:

        The domain is identical/confusingly similar to their trademark.
        You have no legitimate interest in the name.
        You registered it in bad faith (e.g., just to sell it to them for a profit).

        Your Defense: Since you have a functional website with original content and are actively helping the community, you already have a "legitimate interest," making a successful takedown by a "squatter" very difficult.

4.  Registering an Organisation
    You do not need to register an organisation just to hold a domain name.

        When to register: Only register as an NGO (Section 8 company), Trust, or Private Limited if you plan to accept donations, hire employees, or sign formal commercial contracts.
        Does it help? It adds a layer of professionalism and "proof of existence," but a trademark is still a stronger legal tool for domain protection than a simple business registration.

    The Domain: Protected by your active, good-faith use of the site. Secure your login and consider a trademark if you plan to turn this into a major brand.

    To take down or "hijack" a domain, a third party must prove you have no "legitimate interest" in it.

        The Benefit: Having a registered trademark for a logo that prominently features your domain name is concrete legal evidence that you are using the name for a bona fide business or service.
        Domain Disputes: If someone files an INDRP (for .in domains) or UDRP complaint against you, showing a trademark certificate—even for a logo—makes it much harder for them to prove you registered the domain in "bad faith".

2.  It Helps Bypass "Descriptive" Objections
    Since "CPTSD" is a medical term, the Trademark Registry might reject a Wordmark (plain text) application because it is "descriptive" of the condition you are discussing.

        The Benefit: A unique, creative logo is easier to register because the design elements (colors, shapes, icons) make it "distinctive" even if the words themselves are common.
        The Trade-off: Once registered, your protection is strictly tied to that exact visual design. If someone else uses the name "CPTSD.in" but in a completely different font and color, your logo trademark may not be strong enough to stop them.

    If your goal is specifically to stop others from using a similar name and to keep your domain safe, a Wordmark is technically superior.
